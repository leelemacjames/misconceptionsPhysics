IB Physics SL & HL

Common Misconceptions — Organised by Field Type

Gravitational Field

Heavier objects fall fasterSL
All objects accelerate equally in a gravitational field when air resistance is negligible.
No gravity in orbitSL
Gravity is still strong. Astronauts are in free fall, producing apparent weightlessness.
Geostationary satellites feel no gravityHL
They are in circular motion; gravity provides centripetal force.

Electric Field

Current gets used upSL
Charge is conserved. Energy is transferred, not charge.
Voltage flowsSL
Voltage is energy per unit charge, not something that moves.
Electric field lines are particle pathsHL
Field lines represent direction of force, not trajectories.

Magnetic Field

Magnets attract all metalsSL
Only ferromagnetic materials (Fe, Co, Ni).
Stationary charge feels magnetic forceHL
Magnetic force requires motion: F = qvB sinθ.
Magnetic poles exist aloneHL
Magnetic monopoles have not been observed; poles always occur in pairs.

Nuclear Field

Mass is conserved in nuclear reactionsSL
Mass converts to energy via E=mc².
Fusion always releases more energyHL
Energy release depends on binding energy per nucleon curve.
Half-life means half atoms decay each stepSL
Decay is probabilistic; half-life is statistical.

Particle Physics Field

Antimatter is negative matterHL
Antimatter has opposite quantum numbers.
Forces need contactSL
Forces are mediated by exchange particles.
Neutrinos have no massHL
Neutrino oscillations show they have small mass.
