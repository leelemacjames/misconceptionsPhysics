Common Misconceptions — Organised by Field Type

Gravitational Field

Misconception: “Astronauts are weightless because there is no gravity in space.”
Gravity still acts strongly in orbit. Astronauts are in continuous free fall toward Earth, producing apparent weightlessness.
Misconception: “Heavier objects fall faster.”
All objects accelerate at the same rate in a gravitational field when air resistance is negligible.

Electric Field

Misconception: “Current is used up in a resistor.”
Charge is conserved. Current remains the same in a series circuit; energy is transferred, not charge lost.
Misconception: “Voltage flows through a circuit.”
Voltage is energy per unit charge, not a substance. It is a potential difference that drives current.

Magnetic Field

Misconception: “Magnets attract all metals.”
Only ferromagnetic materials (iron, cobalt, nickel) show strong attraction.
Misconception: “A stationary charge in a magnetic field feels a force.”
Magnetic force acts only on moving charges: F = qvB sinθ.

Nuclear Field

Misconception: “Mass is conserved in nuclear reactions.”
Mass converts to energy via E = mc². Mass defect equals binding energy.
Misconception: “Fusion always releases more energy than fission.”
Energy release depends on binding energy per nucleon. Light nuclei fuse; heavy nuclei fission to move toward iron peak.

Particle Physics Field

Misconception: “Antimatter is just negative matter.”
Antimatter particles have opposite quantum numbers, not just opposite charge.
Misconception: “Forces are caused by physical contact.”
Forces are mediated by exchange particles (bosons) in quantum field theory.
